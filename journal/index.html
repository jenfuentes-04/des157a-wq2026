Best Practices for Form Design

January 19th, 2026

The article "Best practices for form design" was surprisingly interesting and helpful to read as a designer. It really made me think more deeply about how much form design affects whether someone actually wants to finish filling it out. If a form feels confusing, too long, or poorly organized, it is very easy for users to give up. People naturally prefer forms that are clear, simple, and easy to understand, which is why design choices matter so much in this context.

Reading through the twenty tips to improve form usability was especially interesting. While I have thought about ways to improve forms before, seeing all the tips clearly explained in one place helped me better understand how small design choices can make a big difference. This article is something I would want to come back to in the future when designing forms. The three tips that stood out to me the most were using multiple steps, clearly explaining why sensitive information is being asked for, and providing a “show password” option. I personally appreciate knowing how many steps a form will take, and I feel more comfortable sharing private information when the reason is clearly stated. Being able to see a password also helps reduce mistakes and frustration.

A website form that I think does a good job following these best practices is Duolingo’s welcome form. The process is straightforward, visually engaging, and easy to complete. It also shows clear progress indicators, which makes the form feel less overwhelming and more enjoyable to finish.
